Unit identifier references (that is, the \token{units} attribute on \Compartment,
the \token{units} attribute on \Parameter, and the \token{substanceUnits}
attribute on \Species) must be the identifier of a \UnitDefinition in the \Model, or the identifier of a predefined unit in SBML, that is, any of the following base units:  
\val{ampere},
\val{becquerel},
\val{candela},
\val{coulomb},
\val{dimensionless},
\val{farad},
\val{gram},
\val{gray},
\val{henry},
\val{hertz},
\val{item},
\val{joule},
\val{katal},
\val{kelvin},
\val{kilogram},
\val{litre},
\val{lumen},
\val{lux},
\val{metre},
\val{mole},
\val{newton},
\val{ohm},
\val{pascal},
\val{radian},
\val{second},
\val{siemens},
\val{sievert},
\val{steradian},
\val{tesla},
\val{volt},
\val{watt}, or
\val{weber}.
(References: L2V4 Section~4.4.2; L2V5 Section~\ref{sec:unit-structure}.)
